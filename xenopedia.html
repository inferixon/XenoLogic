XENOPEDIA // FIELD CATALOG

This archive does not rank species by danger, beauty, or etiquette. It records what they do, how they confuse observers, and why they are useful for logical training.

ROMVESEN // ENTRY 01

Glorp

Known for tapping inspection glass in suspiciously regular patterns. Often appears in the station's training reports because it encourages clean implication chains.

  • Typical behavior - pulses, taps, cobalt static traces
  • Logic value - implication, quantifier traps
  • Threat level - low, unless given office access

ROMVESEN // ENTRY 02

Zaxer

A fast-moving archive pest with a talent for creating false causal stories. Excellent for training analysts not to confuse sequence with proof.

  • Typical behavior - flickers near signal relays
  • Logic value - affirming the consequent traps
  • Threat level - moderate to paperwork

ROMVESEN // ENTRY 03

Plunk

A small lifeform with a dramatic instinct for pressing the wrong button at the perfect moment. Frequently used in protocols about contradiction and insufficient information.

  • Typical behavior - misfires, echoes, panic chirps
  • Logic value - contradiction, missing evidence
  • Threat level - low, but noisy

ROMVESEN // ENTRY 04

Vrex

A territorial observer-class organism that reacts strongly to category errors. Useful for class membership, subclassing, and mistaken generalization examples.

  • Typical behavior - perimeter loops, sharp vocal warnings
  • Logic value - classes, subclasses, scope
  • Threat level - situational
